Day 1
Three Hours & a Dictionary
a dev diary by scar  /  episode 1 of ∞

They say code gets faster the more you write it. Today I learned that's either a lie — or the truth told completely backwards.

overconfidence: a prologue

Woke up feeling dangerous. "I did three challenges yesterday in like five hours?" Thought I was cooking with gas. Thought I had arrived. The to-do list app had other plans.

Add tasks. View tasks. Remove tasks. Dumb simple. The kind of app a bored twelve-year-old builds on a Sunday. Two hours in, I'm staring at this:

ValueError: list.remove(x): x not in list

Why? Because I was mixing lists and dictionaries like a man who doesn't read recipes and then wonders why dinner is wrong. A chaos agent with a keyboard. That was me. For forty-five minutes.

1 / 3
del. three characters. everything.

Googled everything. Tried different approaches. Broke it more. Fixed it slightly. Broke it again with more confidence. Then it landed: stop working with lists. Work with the dictionary.

Found del. Three characters. The whole thing collapsed into place. Task gone. Dictionary clean. Brain finally online. I sat there for a second just blinking at the screen.

the three-hour math problem

Thirty lines of code. Three hours. The ego said: real programmers do this in ten minutes.

Then I remembered — those "real programmers" are either copy-pasting Stack Overflow, lying definitely lying or they've done this exact thing five hundred times already. Me? I hunted. I broke things. I understood why del works on dictionaries but .remove() doesn't. I didn't just write code. I learned it.

Three hours of actual thinking, failing, understanding — that's exactly the pace you need. You know what's actually slow? Learning nothing fast.
— scar
11:53 PM  ·  not racing anymore
2 / 3
the actual realization

Three hours for thirty lines felt like wasting time — until I realized I could explain every single one of them.

Speed was never the point. Understanding is. And understanding doesn't care about your timeline.

That changes everything.
