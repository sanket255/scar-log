Day 2
The Slow Grind
a dev diary by scar  /  episode 2 of ∞

I'm tired. Not the noble kind. Not the "I just ran a marathon and also cured cancer" tired. The "my brain is wet cement and someone is asking it to do calculus" tired.

what actually went down

Woke up feeling cocky. Classic mistake. Hubris is just karma's loading screen. Decided to build a guessing game using while loops — a concept I did not understand, which I thought was a great foundation for building something with it.

One hour. Eight minutes. Twenty lines of code. I did the math. That's 3.26 minutes per line. A typewriter would've beaten me. A broken typewriter.

Somewhere in the middle, I was asking for user input twice by accident. Spent fifteen minutes debugging code that was essentially just… talking too much. Relatable problem. Unfortunate in a program.

Eventually looked at someone else's code, understood it, fixed mine. It worked. Standing ovation. Confetti. A single tear. The guessing game guesses. We are so back.

1 / 3
the thing nobody posts

Everyone online is out here shipping entire SaaS products before lunch. "Built this in 10 minutes." "Shipped this today." Cool. Amazing. I spent an hour on a program whose entire personality is asking "higher or lower?"

If someone handed me that same task right now, I'd still need 45 minutes and I'd still hit the same bug. That's not mastery. That's barely hanging on with both hands.

But here's the thing — nobody posts the hanging-on part. So everybody thinks they're the only one hanging. Great system. Very healthy. No notes for the collective ego of tech Twitter.

on why i stopped

Could I have pushed 4–5 more hours? Sure. Would that have been productive? absolutely probably maybe no. Tired-me codes like a man trying to assemble IKEA furniture during an earthquake. Technically moving. Going nowhere useful.

Yesterday's to-do app took 3 hours. Today it'd take 30 minutes. Not because I got faster. Because my brain cooked it overnight. Sleep is just free compilation time. Someone should've told me earlier.

So I stopped. Intentionally. Strategically. (And also because I was very tired. But the strategic framing feels better.)

the actual realization

An hour and 8 minutes for 20 lines felt embarrassing — until I realized I actually understood all 20 of them. That's not slow. That's just what learning looks like when nobody's performing it for an audience.

Tomorrow will be faster. Not because I'll try harder. Because tonight, my brain is quietly doing the work I can't see.

That's enough. That's the whole thing.
