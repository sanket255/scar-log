Day 3
The Pace Shift
a dev diary by scar  /  episode 3 of ∞

Rest changes everything. Not motivationally. Not philosophically. Just — chemically, measurably, actually. Woke up today and the fog was gone. Didn't earn it. Just slept.

the numbers first, for context

Yesterday: 1 hour 8 minutes for a guessing game. Today: 47 minutes for a calculator, 20 minutes for a palindrome checker. Same brain. One night of sleep. Different human.

That's not speed. That's understanding that finally found somewhere to land.

the calculator, round two

Same concept as yesterday's struggle. But cleaner this time — actually thought through the logic instead of guessing at it and hoping something would compile into dignity.

Changed the loop condition from ask == "y" to ask != "quit". One change. Everything clicked. Nobody told me. I saw it, knew it was wrong, fixed it. That's the difference between copying and learning.

47 minutes. Could've been faster. Didn't try to be. Stopping to think is not the same as going slow. Took me three days to figure that out.

the palindrome wall (2:15 PM)

Started feeling confident. Confidence is just hubris with better posture. Couldn't figure out how to reverse a string. Read some code. Didn't understand it. Built it anyway. Hit a wall. Sat there for 25 minutes just vibing with my own confusion.

Then found an explanation of [::-1]. Not just the syntax — the why. How slicing works. What the negative step actually means. The whole thing laid out like it was obvious, which it was, after 25 minutes of being wrong about it.

Read it. Understood it. Coded it clean in 20 minutes. Struggled for 25. Understood in 20. Will remember forever. If someone had just handed me the answer yesterday when I was tired, I'd have copied it and moved on and forgotten it by morning. The wall was the point.
what day 3 actually means

Day 0 — learning syntax. Day 1 — data structures. Day 2 — loops, barely surviving. Day 3 — actually building things.

Not because I got smarter. Because I let it settle. Rest isn't laziness. It's infrastructure. The code I write tomorrow will be better because I stopped today.
